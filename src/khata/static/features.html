Khata is a privacy-first, self-hosted money-plans and net-worth ledger. Here is exactly what it does — and, just as important, what it does not.

Shared plans & attribution

Invite family members to a plan; every payment is attributed to whoever logged it, and each contributor's ownership share is derived from what they actually paid in.

Limitations Ownership share reflects recorded contributions, not legal title. Adding a member needs their existing account email.

Single source of truth

Every balance — paid-to-date, remaining, interest due, net position — is computed from recorded ledger rows each time you read it. Nothing is stored that could drift out of sync.

Limitations Accuracy depends on what you log. Khata reads no bank feeds in this phase; nothing is inferred automatically.

Money as exact integers

Amounts are stored as integer minor units (paise, cents) and computed with exact decimal arithmetic. Rates are integer basis points. No floating-point money, ever.

Limitations Each plan has one fixed original currency; cross-currency net worth is not yet consolidated.

Asset purchase roll-forward

Set an installment schedule, log payments as they happen, and Khata rolls your total paid forward across the schedule — showing what's covered, what's next, and where you're ahead or behind.

Limitations Payments apply greedily in schedule order; there is no per-payment-to-installment tagging.

Loans with derived interest

Track money lent or borrowed with reducing-balance simple interest, accrued whole-month from the start date. Principal, interest accrued, interest due, and a monthly schedule are all derived.

Limitations Simple interest only (no compounding/EMI amortization yet); unsecured loans only (no collateral in this phase).

Net-position dashboard

One rollup of what you owe, what you're owed, and what you've paid into your asset plans — net position at a glance, with the plans behind it.

Limitations Totals are per-currency sums; mixed-currency portfolios are not yet consolidated into a single figure.

Self-hosted & private

Run Khata on your own machine or server. Your ledger lives in your SQLite database; no third party sees your finances.

Limitations Backups, TLS, and host security are your responsibility — Khata ships sensible defaults, not a managed service.

Sign in with Google

Optionally enable Google sign-in: a one-tap identity check, verified server-side, that links to your account by verified email. Email/password still works without it.

Limitations Identity only — Khata requests no access to your Google data. Self-hosters must configure their own Google client ID; otherwise the button simply does not appear.
