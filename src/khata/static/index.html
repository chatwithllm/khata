The gap

Budgeting apps assume you pay in full, on time, to a bank.

Real life here isn't that. You pay an asset in irregular chunks — cash, a transfer, a loan, a sold asset, a chit payout. You sit in chit funds where the math of pots, bids, dividends and commission never fits a spreadsheet.

"So it all lives in your head — until it doesn't."
What it tracks

Two kinds of money plans, one honest ledger.

Every balance is computed from what you actually recorded — never a stale number you have to trust.

🏠
Asset purchase

Pay it in pieces.

Set the price, sketch a plan, pay when funds arrive. The plan adapts to reality.

  • Installment schedule with roll-forward — a short payment carries to the next, original plan kept.
  • Each payment tagged: cash / transfer, and its funding source.
  • Borrowed to pay? The debt becomes a tracked liability with its own repayment ledger.
  • Attach the receipt, screenshot, or chat — timestamped proof.
🔄
Chit fund · Chitti

Know your real position.

Member or organizer. Pure rotating or auction. The chit math, done for you.

  • Pick your role — member, organizer, or both — per chit.
  • Track contributions, who took the pot, bids & dividends, commission.
  • See your true profit or loss, round by round, not a vague feeling.
  • A chit payout can fund an asset payment — plans connect.
Single source of truth

Every rupee, traceable.

Method, source, exact timestamp, and proof — on every transaction. Attach the screenshot or the chat where the deal was struck. Balances recompute themselves.
